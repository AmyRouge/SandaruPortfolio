Dining Ettiquettes

Lecture Notes

Summary

Dining manners, or etiquette, are important in our daily and professional lives. Etiquette is about showing respect to others and following rules to behave nicely. It helps us build trust and strong relationships. There are different types of etiquette, such as social, religious, dining, phone, and business etiquette. We'll focus on dining etiquette, like how to use utensils, place napkins, and eat politely. We'll also cover social etiquette, like making a good first impression with a smile, warm greeting, and good listening skills.

Lessons Learned

  • Respect for Others: Etiquette is about showing respect to yourself and others, which is important for getting along with people.
  • Building Relationships: Good social behaviors help us build trust and can lead to long-term friendships.
  • Table Manners:
    • Use utensils from the outside in.
    • Put your napkin on your lap and wait for the host to do so first.
    • Break bread into small pieces and use your own butter.
    • Pass salt and pepper together.
    • Take small bites and chew with your mouth closed.
    • Don't blow on hot food.
  • Making a Good First Impression:
    • Smile genuinely and greet warmly.
    • Maintain positive energy and good eye contact.
    • Listen actively.
    • Dress appropriately and take care of your appearance.
  • First Impressions Matter: People judge you quickly, so making a good first impression is important.
