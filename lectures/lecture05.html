Telephone Ettiquettes

Lecture Notes

Summary

The provided guidelines outline the significance of telephone etiquette in business settings, offering fundamental rules for effective communication. This includes speaking directly into the mouthpiece, maintaining courtesy, and promptly answering calls. Proper procedures for placing callers on hold or transferring calls are emphasized, including seeking permission and verification from the caller. Accurate message-taking and professional call return practices are highlighted as essential components. Additionally, the guidelines stress the importance of using appropriate language, tone, and professional closure in conversations.

Lessons Learned

  • Speaking directly into the mouthpiece and maintaining courtesy are fundamental to good telephone etiquette.
  • Answering calls promptly, within 2-3 rings, and greeting callers professionally sets a positive tone for communication.
  • When placing callers on hold, obtaining permission and periodically checking back to avoid prolonged waiting times is essential.
  • Properly transferring calls involves explaining the reason, confirming with the caller, and ensuring the receiving party is ready to take the call.
  • Avoiding vague phrases like "in a meeting" and providing an estimated availability time enhances professionalism.
  • Accurate and confidential message-taking using designated forms is essential for effective communication.
  • Returning calls at a specific time with clear objectives demonstrates professionalism and respect for the caller's time.
  • Using proper language and tone is vital for conveying a positive message and fostering rapport with the caller.
  • Concluding conversations professionally, avoiding slang and negative language, and offering assistance demonstrate commitment to customer service excellence.
  • Utilizing appropriate terminology and refraining from unprofessional expressions contributes to maintaining a professional image in business communication.
