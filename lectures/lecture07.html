Interview Skills

Lecture Notes

Summary

This lesson teaches you how to do well in job interviews. It's important to communicate well, both with words and without words. An interview is a conversation where the interviewer learns about you and you learn about the job and company. You need to listen, ask questions, and answer questions well to do well. This lesson also teaches you how to prepare for an interview, learn about the company, and show confidence.

Lessons Learned

  • Communication is Key: You need to communicate well to do well in an interview.
  • Non-Verbal Cues Matter: Your body language, tone of voice, and eye contact are important.
  • Most Communication is Non-Verbal: 93% of face-to-face communication is non-verbal.
  • Prepare for the Interview: Research the job and company before the interview.
  • Be Ready to Talk: Be prepared to answer questions and ask questions.
  • Show Enthusiasm: Show that you're interested in the job and company.
  • Listen Carefully: Pay attention to what the interviewer is saying.
  • Answer Questions Well: Use the STAR method to answer questions.
  • Describe Yourself: Use good words to describe your personality and strengths.
  • Use the Right Words: Use the present tense to describe your strengths and skills.
  • Say What You Want: Clearly say what you want to achieve in the future.
  • Practice Makes Perfect: Practice interviewing to get better.
