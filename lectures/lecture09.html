Proposal Writing

Lecture Notes

Summary

A project proposal is a detailed plan that explains a project idea or solution. It takes a lot of time and effort to write a good proposal. The goal is to convince the reader to accept the idea or solution. There are two main types of proposals: external and internal. External proposals are used to bid for contracts, while internal proposals are used to get approval within an organization. External proposals have three main parts: a summary, a management plan, and a budget. Internal proposals need to be planned and written carefully, considering the audience and how to solve the problem.

Lessons Learned

  • Types of Proposals: Know the difference between external and internal proposals and who they are for.
  • External Proposals: Include a summary, management plan, and budget in external proposals.
  • Planning Internal Proposals: Think about who will read the proposal and use pictures to make it clear.
  • Structuring Internal Proposals: Start with a clear introduction that explains the purpose, problem, and solution.
  • Persuading the Reader: Use detailed explanations to convince the reader that your solution is the best.
