Email Ettiquettes

Lecture Notes

Summary

The provided guidelines offer advice on effective professional email communication, highlighting the significance of utilizing a professional email account, establishing a signature, and ensuring clarity and specificity in the subject line. Additionally, the guidelines address email etiquette, including appropriate salutations and closings, maintaining politeness and consideration, and refraining from excessive use of exclamation marks. Furthermore, it offers recommendations on follow-up emails and understanding email abbreviations, as well as notifying about absences.

Lessons Learned

  • Employing a professional email account is imperative for both academic and professional correspondence.
  • Creating a signature containing your name and contact details is indispensable for professional email correspondence.
  • Subject lines should be concise and specific, incorporating logical keywords to aid the recipient in promptly grasping the email's intent.
  • Adhering to appropriate salutations and closings, such as "Dear [Name]" and "Best regards," is crucial for maintaining professionalism in emails.
  • Exercising politeness and thoughtfulness in email requests is vital for fostering a positive tone in communication.
  • Follow-up emails may be necessary for ensuring a response, but demonstrating patience is essential.
  • Familiarizing oneself with common email abbreviations, such as ASAP and FYI, can enhance communication efficiency.
  • Avoiding excessive use of exclamation marks is advisable, as it can detract from the professionalism of the email.
  • When notifying about absences, providing brief and concise details is preferable.
  • Scheduled appointments do not qualify as excused absences, necessitating clear communication regarding availability.
